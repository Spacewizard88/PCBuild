Come with me as I explain my 2 year old future proof gaming PC that was built during the covid lockdown

Why a 2020 PC build?

In this build breakdown I will be talking about the hardware that is currently in my PC and why I chose them. But here's a little background story on how it came to be. It's october of 2020. Just 8 months after the world changing event of the COVID-19 outbreak. Everyone is stuck at home quarantining but with so much time on my hands I decided to explore some new hobbies and that's when I came across the world of building PC's. Before I could even think about building my own PC, I needed to learn and understand how a PC works. A popular Reddit community by the name of r/pcmasterrace and a handful of Youtubers are what made me a master of PC's in just 2 weeks. My two biggest problems at the time were the scarcity of computer parts and the absoult insane price markup on the ones that weren't sold out. But I prevailed and managed to get greats deals on my specs. Now enough talking, let's dive right into it!

CPU: AMD Ryzen 5 3600

A CPU, or central processing unit, is the brain of the computer. Like a human brain, the CPU controls all the functions of the computer. This is the AMD Ryzen 5 3600. It's a pretty solid CPU. It's a couple years old and I am able to stream games at max settings as well as render video. It stays pretty cool and is worth the money. I don't see myself needing to upgrade anytime soon.

CPU Cooler: Wraith Stealth

Now for the CPU cooler. This is an important part because it is what will take some of the heat away from your hardworking CPU. Without it, your CPU will overheat. I decided to go with the Wraith Stealth cooler which already comes in the box. It does exactly what it needs to do and does a great job keeping the CPU temperature low. I don't overclock or continuously work under a heavy load, so it made perfect sense to keep the stock cooler.

Motherboard: ROG STRIX B450-F

The motherboard is like the brain of the computer. All of the different parts of the computer are connected to the motherboard.This lets them work together. Your CPU sits in the motherboard and all your external peripherals (Keyboard,Mouse,Ethetnet) connect. This motherboard has an AM4 socket which is compatible to Ryzen 5000, 3rd/2nd/1st Gen AMD Ryzen CPUs. I liked that it had two HDMI ports as well as a numch of USB ports. It also runs pretty fast. My only regret would be that it didn't come with in board WIFI.

Memory: Corsair Vengeance RGB pro 16gb

Memory is where information is stored for immediate use. The memory I chose was the Corsair vengeance RGB pro 16gb. It comes with 2 in the box. Each stick is 8gb. I personally only selected it because of the RGB. But its good for the price and runs fast.

Storage: Seagate Barracuda 2TB internal hard drive & WD Blue SN550 NVMe SSD 500gb

Storage gives a computer the ability to retain data such as documents, photos, videos etc. I have two different forms of storage for my computer. The first one being the Seagate Barracuda external hard drive. I purposely chose for it to have 2TB because I knew Id be downloading a lot of games, apps and photos. I think its a great hard drive for the amount of storage and the price. It also runs fast. My second form of storage is the WD Blue SN550 SSD. This storage sits on the motherboard and has 500gb. The SSD is much quicker than the external hard drive. I only have my OS and a couple huge games that are normally known to take a while to load.

Graphics Card: Nvidia GeForce RTX 2060 Super

The graphics card is what sends a video signal to your display. At the time of the pandemic, it was super hard to get the new 30 series card or any card for that matter because building a computer became a trend. And because it was so popular, everyone was buying everything, and prices were through the roof because of all the demand and very little product available. I chose the 2060 Super because I was looking for something that wouldnt phase out and would last a long time before needing an upgrade. I also wanted something super reliable because if I needed to have it replaced that would be a problem. The 2060 Super has 8gb of GDDR6 RAM and it still puts out a great picture when playing AAA games.

Case: NZXT H510

The case is the unit that holds all the computers components in one place. The H510 is a mid-tower case. I bought it for aesthetic purposes, but the downside is the lack of ventilation in the case. Down the road I will probably end up switching cases for the sake of keeping all my components cooled. Other than that, its a wonderful case for the price.
